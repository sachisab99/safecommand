## Deck principle — three-step closing

Each slide answers one question:
1. *Why does Apollo board care about cross-venue safety today?*  → **The Governance Problem**
2. *What is the unique product capability SafeCommand offers?*  → **Enterprise Brand Enablement**
3. *Show me what it actually looks like.*  → **Live Demo**

Slides land in this order because:
- Slide 1 establishes pain (governance dashboard is the hook — Apollo's board cannot answer "which of our 65 hospitals has a fire-NOC gap right now")
- Slide 2 introduces the differentiator (white-label = Apollo's brand on SafeCommand's platform)
- Slide 3 proves capability with live software (Path C mockup — the Loom)

If any single slide is cut, the conversation still closes — slide 3 alone (the Loom + verbal narrative) carries 70% of the conversion power.

## SLIDE 1 — The Governance Problem

### Visual
- **Top-left**: Apollo logo (small, branded — establishes context immediately)
- **Top-right**: SafeCommand wordmark (small, gray — the vendor relationship is acknowledged but not dominant)
- **Centre**: a single rhetorical question, large type
- **Below**: a visualisation of the 65-hospital governance challenge

### Headline
> *"Right now, can your board answer: Which of your 65 hospitals has a Fire-NOC gap, an overdue evacuation drill, or an open incident — at this exact moment?"*

### Sub-headline (smaller, beneath)
> *Most CXOs answer: 'It would take days. We would call each hospital individually.'*

### Visual element below
A muted India map with 65 hospital pins. Colour every pin a single neutral grey. **No data shown.** This communicates: today, your dashboard is a list of names, not a real-time pulse.

### Bullet points (4 lines max — bottom of slide)
- 65 hospitals across 12 Indian states
- Per-hospital safety reports: filed annually, per local NABH cycle, in 65 different formats
- Group-level safety posture: assembled manually each quarter
- Incident response: depends on which hospital's GM happened to escalate, when they escalated, to whom

### Talking points (founder reads aloud, 90 seconds)
1. *"Your group has spent 15 years building India's strongest hospital brand. Apollo Hospitals is shorthand for 'hospital that does this safely'."*
2. *"And yet — if a Fire Brigade officer in Hyderabad called your group office today asking which Apollo hospital missed its last NABH drill, you'd need to call 65 hospitals to find out."*
3. *"That's not a technology problem. That's a governance gap. And it's a gap your insurers, your regulators, and your board have noticed."*
4. *"SafeCommand closes that gap in 30 seconds. Drillable to the specific zone causing the issue."*
5. *"Let me show you how — first the platform layer, then the brand layer."*

## Design notes

| Element | Spec |
|---|---|
| Slide aspect ratio | 16:9 (works for Keynote, Slides, PowerPoint, Loom embed) |
| Heading typography | Inter Bold or system equivalent; 36–44pt |
| Body typography | Inter Regular; 18–22pt |
| Apollo red `#C8102E` | Primary brand accent on slide 2 (split-frame right side) and slide 3 callouts |
| Apollo navy `#002F6C` | Secondary brand accent; slide 1 headline emphasis |
| WCAG validation | All foreground text on each background must pass 4.5:1 contrast (auto-validated for these colour pairs — Apollo red on white = 5.88:1; Apollo navy on white = 12.94:1) |
| Slide 3 Loom embed | Recorded post-Phase B; 3 minutes; 1080p; private link |
| Footer disclaimer | Every slide; 9–10pt grey (#666); not white/light to avoid contrast failure on light backgrounds |

- **Apollo CXO meeting (60 min)** — show all 3 slides; Loom on slide 3
- **Apollo procurement screen** — slide 1 + slide 2 only; Loom on a follow-up
